<h3>Question:</h3>

How does being parasitized affect the stress responses of H. oregonensis to extended exposure to increased temperature?


 <h3>Hypothesis: 	</h3>

Parasitism will decrease the ability of H. oregonensis to respond to environmental stressors.


<h3>Methods:</h3>

1. Prepare 2 tanks for experimental testing, 35 ppt salinity and 24°C
2. Separate crabs between parasitized and non-parasitized, marking each with bee markers and superglue
3. Take mass (g) of each crab using metric scale and plastic weigh boat
4. Take righting time of each crab by flipping it upside down and measuring reaction with phone stopwatch (s)
5. Place green crab in tray, sectioned off by plastic mesh. Place 1.5 cm² grid underneath to allow distance measurement (units/s)
6. Test each experimental crab's reactivity to predator presence via distance over 5 seconds
  6.1 European green crab should be placed and held down from the top, green shore crab placed at (6, 3) of a 11 x 15 grid
  6.2 Test should be run in wet and dry conditions, with wet conditions having enough water to submerge green shore crabs
7. Place crabs in tanks, separated by parasitized status
8. Repeat steps 3 and 4 once a week for 2 consecutive weeks
9. After 3 weeks in the experimental setting, repeat steps 3-6
10. Conduct hemolymph extractions
11. Replace crabs in control tank
13. Run hemolymph osmolality (mmol/kg)
